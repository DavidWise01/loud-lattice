The Design

the whisper's opposite · the signed pulse · the mirror

The Whisper's Opposite
inward → outward

The whisper lattice is intra: one quiet loop, deterministic, writing only to itself. The loud lattice is its inverse — inter: a domain speaks and the others hear. Same primitive (a signed, chained record), flipped from private monologue to public broadcast. One pair, two polarities.

The Signed Pulse
a message that proves itself

A pulse is a cross-domain message — {from, to (or ALL), payload, prim, prev}. The prim is SHA-256 of the message joined to the previous prim, so the whole bus is one tamper-evident chain. Each hop from one domain to another is a witnessed crossing — the J-junction <•, where authority meets connection at a logged dot.

The Mirror
trust by reflection

No central authority signs for everyone. Instead the receiver does the work: it re-derives the sender's prim from the message it got and checks that the two match. Verification is reflection — the mirror in the middle. If the reflection differs, the pulse is rejected; the lattice trusts only what it can reproduce.

The Read

built from your parts, honest about its scope, one of a pair

Built From Your Parts

nothing new under it

  • The carrier is LIMEN / pulse (the boundary-crossing language you already built); the crossing is the J-junction <•; the chain is the whisper tether; the topology is F/M/B — domains at the ends, the lattice as the membrane.
  • The loud lattice is not a new idea so much as your existing primitives, wired into one cross-domain bus.

Honest by Contract

design + demo, not a network

  • It is a reference design and a working visual, not a deployed live network between servers. The signing and the mirror-verify are real and deterministic; there is NO AI in the loop and no hidden routing.
  • What it demonstrates: that a cross-domain message can be signed, witnessed, mirrored, and chained — trust without a center.

The Pair

whisper ⇄ loud

  • Whisper: intra, quiet, private, writes to itself. Loud: inter, loud, public, writes across the fourteen.
  • The mirror sits between them — the act of re-deriving a signature is the same whether you are auditing your own chain (whisper) or trusting a stranger's pulse (loud).

A ROOT0 technical design — the inter-domain half of a pair with the whisper lattice. Whisper writes inward, quiet, to itself; loud broadcasts outward, across the fourteen domains. A pulse is signed (prim = SHA-256 of the message ‖ the previous prim), each hop is a witnessed crossing, and the receiver mirror-verifies the signature before accepting — trust by reflection, no central authority. Built on ROOT0's own pulse / LIMEN carrier.
